# Emergency Numbers & Healthcare

Essential contacts and information for staying safe in Sicily.

## Emergency Services

**112** - European Emergency Number (All services)
- Works across EU, connects to police, fire, or ambulance
- English-speaking operators usually available

**113** - Police (Polizia di Stato)
**115** - Fire Department (Vigili del Fuoco)
**118** - Ambulance (Ambulanza)

## Healthcare

### Pharmacies (Farmacia)
- Marked by green cross
- Many open late or on rotation for emergencies
- Basic medical advice often available
- Look for "Farmacia di Turno" signs for after-hours service

### Finding Medical Care
- **Guardia Medica**: Night/weekend doctor service (dial 118 or local number)
- **Pronto Soccorso**: Emergency room (free for urgent care with EU health card)
- Bring European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) if from EU

## Tourist Police
In major cities, dedicated tourist police can help with:
- Lost/stolen documents
- Tourist-related crimes
- Language assistance

**Palermo**: Via Generale Magliocco
**Catania**: Piazza Duomo area

## Consular Services
Keep your embassy/consulate number saved:
- Can replace lost passports
- Assist in emergencies
- Provide local lawyer contacts

## Safety Tips
- Keep copies of documents separate from originals
- Note location of nearest pharmacy when you arrive
- Save emergency numbers in your phone before you need them
- Most tourist areas are very safe, but stay alert in crowded places
